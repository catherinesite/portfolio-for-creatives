  • Editorial Cover

    This cover illustration depicts Europe’s energy dependence on Russia. The pipes at the base of the Kremlin, an iconic image of Russia, represent different European flags. The ominous background suggests the threat of running out energy altogether.

  • Souvenir Bag

    This souvenir bag depicts the national bird of El Salvador and the traditional art of the country. I chose vibrant colours to show the lively spirit of the culture and people.

  • Concept Art

    The inspiration for this piece came from thinking, ‘if gods had their own kingdom, what would it look like?’ I wanted to include a variety of temples. The light colours and clouds are meant to convey that this place is ‘Heavenly.’

  • Book Cover

    Illustration for House of Leaves by Mark Z. Danielewski. The book presents readers with a story within a story. The cover scene shows a main character, Navidson, as he leads his team into the mysterious hallway. The pages flying across the back and front of the cover represent the many layers of the story.

  • About Me

    Catherine Rosales is a fourth year student in the IDEA School of Design at Capilano University. Born in El Salvador, she moved to Maryland at the age of 10 and continues to be fluent in both cultures and languages. Catherine has always loved telling stories, whether with words or illustrations and continues to believe in the magic of evoking emotions through art. Creating her own graphic novel is number one in her bucket list. In the meantime, she plans to keep offering her illustrative skills to the world. Thank you for your visit! You can connect with Catherine by sending an email to: catherinerosales@my.capilanou.ca
